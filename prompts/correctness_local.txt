The transformer architecture processes sequences of tokens by first projecting each token into a high-dimensional embedding space, then passing the resulting vectors through alternating layers of multi-head self-attention and feed-forward networks. Each attention layer computes scaled dot-product attention across all positions in the sequence, allowing the model to aggregate information from distant tokens. Mixture-of-experts layers replace the dense feed-forward block with a learned router that selects a sparse subset of expert networks for each token, reducing the number of active parameters per forward pass while retaining the total parameter count of a much larger model.
